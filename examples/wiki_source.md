# Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is a biological process used by most plants, algae, and some bacteria to convert **light energy** into *chemical energy* stored in glucose.

## Overview

It is fundamental to life on Earth, producing the oxygen we breathe and forming the base of most food chains.

## Chemical Equation

The overall chemical equation for photosynthesis is:

```
6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
```

## Stages

Photosynthesis occurs in two main stages:

1. Light-dependent reactions
2. Light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle)

### Light-Dependent Reactions

The light-dependent reactions occur in the **thylakoid membranes** of the chloroplast. These reactions capture light energy and convert it to chemical energy in the form of `ATP` and `NADPH`.

### Calvin Cycle

The Calvin cycle takes place in the **stroma** of the chloroplast.

- Carbon fixation by [RuBisCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RuBisCO)
- Reduction of 3-PGA to G3P
- Regeneration of RuBP

## Factors Affecting Photosynthesis

| Factor | Effect | Optimal Range |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Light intensity | Increases rate up to saturation point | 10,000-12,000 lux |
| Temperature | Enzyme activity peaks then declines | 25-35°C |
| CO₂ concentration | Rate increases with concentration | 0.04-0.4% |

## Ecological Importance

Photosynthesis is arguably the most important biochemical process on Earth, responsible for producing virtually all atmospheric oxygen.

> "The oxygen you're breathing right now was produced by photosynthesis."
